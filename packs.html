What muscles do crunches work?

As a core exercise, crunches target your abdominal muscles. More specifically, it works your rectus abdominis, the six-pack muscle, and obliques. They’re an extremely effective bodyweight ab exercise that helps build a stronger core. When done correctly, they target both your upper and lower abdominals.

Flutter kicks

How do you do flutter kicks? To do flutter kicks, Sklar recommended laying down on your back on a comfortable, flat surface, like a yoga mat. Then, keeping your legs straight, scissor your legs up and down about six to eight inches apart while pressing your lower back into the floor.


Leg raise

How do you do a leg raise? For this how-to, we’re going to discuss the most common leg raise variation. For that, you'll place the arms by the sides of your body. To begin this exercise, lie supine (flat on your back) and extend the legs. Extend your arms and place them by the sides of the body, then lightly press the hands into the ground.

Sit-up

How to do a sit-up? Focus on flexing the abdominals throughout the full range of motion, starting from beginning of the lift off to at the top of the exercise. Once you have made it to the top of the sit-up, you need to then lower yourself down with complete control.

How to do cross crunches?

1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent. 2. Cross your right leg on top of the left knee, and support your head with the left hand. 3. Crunch and bring your left elbow across your body and toward the right knee. 4. Repeat for as many reps as you can and switch sides. When doing cross crunches make sure that your abs are doing all the work.

Elbow plank

How to do planks with palms and forearms? Planks using either your palms or forearms are both excellent exercises. • Begin in a plank position, with your forearms and toes facing down on the floor. • Your elbows are just under your shoulders, and your forearms are facing forward. • Keep your head relaxed, and stare down at the floor.
